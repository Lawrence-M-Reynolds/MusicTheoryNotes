A major triad with a minor third on top to form give the 7th:

G - (M3) - B - (m3) - D - (m3) - F

**Major Scale**
This only occurs on the V chord in major. And it resolves to the tonic chord:

G->E (root demoted to the fith)
B->C (leading)
D->D
F->E  (leading)

**Minor Scale**
This occurs on the V chord of the minor scale with the raised 3rd:

E - (M3) - G# - (m3) - B - (m3) - D

E -> E
G# -> A
B -> C
D-> E

But it can also occur on the VII:
G - (M3) - B - (m3) - D - (m3) - F
And in exactly the same way, this can modulate into the relative major scale.
