A major triad with a minor third on top to form give the 7th:

G - (M3) - B - (m3) - D - (m3) - F

Note that the 3rd and 7th form the [[Tritone]]. As a result, this chord has a pull for the 3rd to resolve up and the 7th to resolve down. In both cases this could be either a minor second, or major second.

In the case of moving by a major second you introduce foreign tones. These 

## Examples
**Major Scale**
This only occurs on the V chord in major. And it resolves to the tonic chord:

G->E (root demoted to the fifth)
B->C (leading)
D->D
F->E  (leading)

**Minor Scale**
This occurs on the V chord of the minor scale with the raised 3rd:

E - (M3) - G# - (m3) - B - (m3) - D

E -> E
G# -> A
B -> C
D-> E

But it can also occur on the VII:
G - (M3) - B - (m3) - D - (m3) - F
And in exactly the same way, this can modulate into the relative major scale.

## Secondary dominant
when you build a non-diatonic dominant 7th chord on any other tone than the fifth of the scale. You're basically just using the V7 chord of another scale and it can be used to resolve to the tonic of the new key, modulating to that key.
